\section{Introduction-----THESE SECTIONS ARE NOT READY, JUST DRAFT}



The integration of robotics and artificial intelligence into healthcare is a rapidly advancing field, driven by global challenges such as aging populations, rising healthcare costs, and shortages of medical personnel. In response, researchers have developed various robotic solutions, broadly categorized into telepresence robots for remote consultation, autonomous robots for logistics, and socially assistive robots (SARs) for patient engagement. While each category has demonstrated significant value independently, they often operate in silos, addressing only a fraction of the holistic needs of patients and clinicians. This review surveys the current state of these technologies to identify the critical research gap that necessitates the development of an integrated, intelligent healthcare robot. The aim is to demonstrate that while the components for such a system exist, their synthesis into a single, user-friendly, and autonomous platform remains an underexplored and highly impactful area of research, particularly for addressing healthcare accessibility challenges like those in Oman.
