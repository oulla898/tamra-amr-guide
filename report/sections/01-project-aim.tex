Healthcare in Oman faces a simple but serious challenge: doctors cannot always be where patients need them. This affects everyone - elderly people who cannot travel to hospitals, children in rural areaswho need specialist care, and patients in remote communities where the nearest doctor might be hours away. While Oman’s healthcare system reaches 100\% of urban families, 10\% of rural families still lack adequate access to medical care.When doctors are unavailable due to emergencies, travel, or simply being in another location, patients wait. Sometimes they wait too long. This is particularly difficult for elderly patients who may have mobility issues, and for children who need comfort and reassurance during medical situations.[1]
