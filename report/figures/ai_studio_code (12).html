The Challenge

The Burden: Nurses spend up to 30% of their shifts on non-clinical logistics (transporting labs, meals, linens).

The Risk: Routine tasks increase exposure to pathogens and Hospital Acquired Infections (HAIs).

The Gap:

  • Existing logistics robots lack empathy.
  • Telepresence robots require manual driving.
  • No unified solution for Omani hospitals.

Contrast: Industrial efficiency (Amazon) vs. Current Hospital reality

Slide 4 / 11

Design Selection: AHP Analysis

We used the Analytic Hierarchy Process to objectively select the robot platform.

The Winning Choice: Hybrid

Why Hybrid?
It balances Reliability (using a commercial base for movement) with Innovation (custom AI layer for interaction).

  • Alternative A (Commercial): High reliability, but closed system (No AI customization).
  • Alternative B (DIY): High flexibility, but low mechanical reliability.
  • Alternative C (Hybrid): Best of both worlds.

Slide 6 / 11

Navigation: LiDAR SLAM

The Concept: Sensor Fusion

We utilize ROS 2 Nav2 Stack combining:

  • Lidar: For mapping static obstacles (Walls).
  • Odometry/IMU: For estimating robot movement.
  • Local Planner: For dynamic obstacle avoidance (People).

Simulation: Mapping & Raycasting

Live simulation: Robot (Blue) scanning environment (Red Points)

Slide 7 / 11

Precision Localization

Beyond SLAM, we investigated RF technologies for high-precision scenarios.

Multilateration: Uses distance from anchors to triangulate position.

Angle of Arrival (AoA):

Allows the robot to "home in" on a beacon (e.g., a doctor's badge) without a pre-existing map.

Slide 8 / 11

Intelligence & HRI

The Brain: Google Gemini 3 Flash

We selected Gemini 3 Flash over GPT-4 based on our analysis:

  • Latency: Faster response time (critical for conversation).
  • Cost: Free tier availability for development.
  • Multimodal: Capable of processing visual inputs.

Black Box View: Transforming unstructured Voice/Touch inputs into structured robotic actions.
